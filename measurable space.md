## Definition ([[Bas2024]], pg. 11): measurable space
The pair $(X, \mathcal{A})$, where $X$ is a set and $\mathcal{A}$ is a [[σ-algebra]], is called a [[measurable space]].

## Definition ([[Kal2021]], pg. 10): measurable space
A measurable space is a pair $(S, \mathcal{S})$, where $S$ is a space and $\mathcal{S}$ is a [[σ-field]] in $S$.
